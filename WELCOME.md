# Welcome — your AI is about to actually know you

You just installed **LifeOS** (also called PAI — Personal AI Infrastructure). Most AI assistants forget
you the moment a chat ends. This one doesn't. It keeps a small set of files about who you are, what
you're working toward, and how you like to work — and it reads them every time, so it can help you like
something that actually knows you, not a stranger you re-explain yourself to every day.

Those files start out empty. **Filling them in is the whole point of the first hour.** This guide walks
you through it.

---

## The big idea in one picture

```
   YOU  ──talk about your life──▶  a set of plain files (your "TELOS")  ──read every session──▶  YOUR AI
```

- **TELOS** is just a fancy word for the files that describe you: your mission, your goals, the
  problems you care about, what's in your way, and the life you're aiming for.
- Your assistant reads them automatically. You never have to re-explain your goals — it already knows.
- You own the files. They live on your computer. You can read and edit them any time.

## Your first hour, in four steps

### Step 1 — Give your assistant a name and a personality (5 min)

Right now your assistant is nameless. You get to name it and decide how it talks to you — dry and
blunt, warm and encouraging, formal, funny, whatever fits you. You'll do this in the interview (Step 2),
so you don't need to decide anything yet. Just know it's yours to shape.

### Step 2 — Do the voice interview (25 min) ← the important one

This is the part that makes everything else work. Instead of typing answers into a form, you'll **talk**
— to a voice AI, like a good conversation with a curious friend. It asks about your goals and your life;
you answer out loud; at the end it hands you a block of text.

**How:**
1. Open a **new chat**. Best options, in order: **ChatGPT** (handles long sessions best), then
   **Claude** (use a normal chat, *not* a "Project"), then **Gemini** (fine for the short version).
2. Open **`INTERVIEW-PACKET.md`** (it came with this guide), select all of its text, and **paste the
   text** into the chat as your first message. Paste the text rather than attaching the file — pasted
   text is the only method that reliably works once you switch to voice.
3. Turn on **voice mode** and say **"preflight."** The AI should reply with a codeword, `TELOS-READY`,
   plus a quick plan. If it does, you're good — say **"begin."** If it *doesn't* say the codeword, it
   didn't read the packet; paste the packet text into the chat as a message and try again.
4. Talk for about 25 minutes. Answer honestly, skip anything you don't want to share ("pass" always
   works), and let it follow interesting tangents — that's where the good stuff comes from.
5. When you're done, say **"give me the dump."** It produces one big block of text. **Copy all of it.**

> **A note on privacy:** this happens inside Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini, so what you say is stored there like
> any other chat. It will never ask for passwords or account numbers. Skip anything that feels too
> personal — you can always add it later, privately, on your own computer.

### Step 3 — Hand the dump to your assistant (5 min)

Now go to your assistant **in the terminal** (where you installed LifeOS). Paste the block you copied,
and say something like:

> "Here's my completed onboarding interview — please read it in."

Your assistant knows what to do: it reads your answers into your TELOS files, saves a backup first, and
sets everything up. You just watch. If it asks you a question or two to clear something up, answer it.

### Step 4 — See it come alive (2 min)

Open your dashboard (your assistant can show you — it's usually called **Pulse**). The empty rings and
placeholders should now be full of *you*. That's the moment it clicks: the system knows who you are.

## What if I want to go deeper?

The 25-minute version (called **Express**) covers the essentials and is genuinely enough to start. But
the interview has two longer tiers:

- **Standard (~45 min)** adds how you think and talk — your strategies, your beliefs, the way you frame
  your work.
- **Deep (~90 min)** adds everything — creative pursuits, formative experiences, your predictions, and
  your tastes in books, music, and food.

You don't do these all at once. Come back another day, open a fresh chat, paste the packet *and* your
last dump, and it'll pick up where you left off.

## Frequently asked

**Do I have to use voice?** No — you can type your answers in the same chat. But voice is easier and
tends to get richer, more honest answers, because talking is lower-effort than typing for most people.

**Which AI is best for this?** Any of the three works. Whichever you already pay for and like talking to
is the right one. The packet's "preflight" check confirms it's working before you invest real time.

**What if I get interrupted halfway?** Say "give me the dump" whenever you need to stop. Hand back what
you have; you can finish later. Nothing is lost.

**Can I edit the files myself afterward?** Yes, always. They're plain text on your computer. Or just
tell your assistant "update my goals" any time — it'll walk you through it.

**Is my information sent anywhere?** The interview chat lives with whichever AI company you used (Claude,
OpenAI, or Google), like any chat. But your TELOS files themselves live **only on your computer** —
nothing about them is uploaded unless you choose to.

**What's the difference between LifeOS and PAI?** Same system. It was called PAI (Personal AI
Infrastructure); newer versions are called LifeOS. This packet works with both.

You get out what you put in. Twenty-five honest minutes of talking about your actual life turns a
generic chatbot into something that understands what you're trying to do. That's the trade. It's a good
one.
